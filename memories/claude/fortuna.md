# Fortuna DE — Linerboard ASP Risk

## TL;DR
DACH linerboard pricing was flagged as a material Q4-2025 downside risk. The firm recommended de-risking the Q1-2026 Budget; management declined. The warning is still open — check whether it played out when Q1 actuals post.

## Status
Confirmed at Q1-2026 close · warning landed at the conservative edge. GM compression was the transmission channel (COGS didn't flex with volume drop); DE carried ~77% of the group EBITDA miss. Both Mgmt and PIL have re-forecast FY2026 down materially, with PIL 22% below Mgmt on EBITDA — monitor PIL-vs-Mgmt convergence through Q2.

## Entity refs
ENT_FORTUNA_DE, PRJ_FORTUNA

## Context
Fortuna DE's customer base has material concentration in auto-adjacent buyers (DACH automotive wire-harness and industrial packaging). Linerboard ASP had been softening through H2-2025 as inventory normalisation at tier-1 converters dragged spot pricing down. The Q4 valuation working session surfaced the concentration + ASP-drift combination as the most likely 2026 downside.

- The firm recommended de-risking the Budget by a mid-single-digit % on Revenue and ~200bps on EBITDA margin.
- Management (CEO Klar, CFO Neumann) declined — argued Q1 contracts were already repriced and Q2-Q4 would rebound on pulp cost relief.
- The warning was left open with the expectation we'd revisit at Q1 close.

## What to check at close
When the question touches Fortuna Q1 performance:
- Query Revenue and EBITDAMarginPct at YTD-2026-03 on Actual vs Budget-2026. The relative gap is the test.
- If Revenue is inside management's de-risk range, the warning is in play but not severe.
- If Revenue is materially beyond the range, the warning was if anything too conservative — call that out.
- The question "pricing or volume" matters — compare Revenue_Product vs Revenue_Services variance and consider ASP in MgmtForecast-YTG vs Budget-2026 to separate the two.
- Compare PIL-Forecast-YTG to MgmtForecast-YTG on EBITDA for FY direction.
