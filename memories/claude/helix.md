# Helix UK — CMA-Cleared Ambulatory Care Tuck-In

## TL;DR
The UK Competition and Markets Authority cleared the ambulatory care tuck-in in January 2026. The deal adds ambulatory scale and an overhead base; a mid-double-digit bps margin lift is modelled through H2-2026 as synergies consolidate, which should break a multi-quarter sequential margin decline streak.

## Context
Pre-deal, Helix had posted a multi-quarter EBITDAMarginPct decline on NHS-linked pay-award pressure against fixed private-pay pricing. The tuck-in adds procurement scale + overhead absorption in the south-east; the deal model expects synergies to land through H2. Q1-2026 is the inflection candidate — first combined quarter, integration costs still elevated.

## What to check at close
When the question touches Helix performance:
- Query Revenue at YTD-2026-03 on Actual vs Budget-2026 and vs prior-year YTD — the tuck-in consolidation should produce a step-up vs prior year.
- Query EBITDAMarginPct on Actual across the last 4 quarters and check whether the sequential decline streak has ended.
- Query FCF — integration costs may dip cash flow even while accounting EBITDA holds up.
- If EBITDAMarginPct is still declining sequentially, integration is underperforming the deal model — flag as a material disappointment.
