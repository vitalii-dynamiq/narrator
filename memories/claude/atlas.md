# Atlas NL — Logistics Comp-Set Re-Rating

## TL;DR
Lazard's logistics comp-set update (Feb-2026) flagged a sector re-rate of European 3PL multiples on the back of last-mile roll-ups and demand tailwind. Atlas's Q1 FV story is expected to be multiple-driven, not performance-driven.

## Status
Open · expected to resolve at V2 close.

## Entity refs
ENT_ATLAS_NL, PRJ_ATLAS

## Context
Lazard's comp set: Kuehne+Nagel, DSV, GXO, DB Schenker (pre-carve-out), EU 3PL-weighted warehousing REITs. Multiple expansion is the structural tailwind — sector fundamentals improved; Atlas's own LTM operating performance is broadly to plan, maybe modestly up.

Working capital was expected to tighten (normalisation after a H2-2025 receivables build) which would also move Net Debt.

## What to check at close
When the question touches Atlas valuation:
- Query FairValue at Valuation-V1 (2025-12) and Valuation-V2 (2026-03).
- Walk the bridge: ebitdaEffect, multipleEffect, leverageEffect, fxEffect, crossTerm, otherEffect.
- The thesis expects **multipleEffect** to be the dominant leg.
- LTM EBITDA delta should be modest → ebitdaEffect secondary.
- Net Debt step-up or step-down maps to leverageEffect with sign flipped.
- Report which leg actually dominated — if it's not multipleEffect, something's different from the Lazard thesis.

## Observation (V2 close)
Multiple effect did dominate as expected (sector re-rate leg). However, Net Debt moved up at V2 rather than tightening as thesis anticipated — worth probing WC (receivables build) with management before V3. Leverage leg therefore subtracted from FV instead of contributing.
